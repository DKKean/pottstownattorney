Awards and recognitions

The firm’s recognitions, explained in plain language, without overstatement.

Why this page exists

Clients are right to be skeptical of legal marketing. Some firms display every online “badge” they can buy, while downplaying what actually happens in a courtroom. Here, the point is simple: understand what a rating means before you treat it as proof of anything in your case.

Read alongside experience

Peer review and public profiles are one type of signal. They are not a substitute for talking through your facts, your charges, and the risks you care about. If you are comparing lawyers, use this page to separate serious recognition from noise — then call and ask how your case would be worked up.

Accreditation graphics in use on this site

Supplied and approved for web use. Each mark remains the property of its owner; display follows the firm’s current profiles. Ratings are not a promise of a future result.

Peer review (AV Preeminent)

Confidential input from judges and lawyers who work in the same profession. It says something about how colleagues view ability and ethics—it is not a consumer poll.

Client and directory ratings

Public profiles reflect who chose to post a review, on that platform’s rules. Useful context, not a complete census of every past client.

AV Preeminent (Martindale-Hubbell)

AV Preeminent is a peer review distinction from Martindale-Hubbell. Reviewers in the profession rate colleagues on legal ability and professional standards. It is not a “pay to play” internet badge. Martindale-Hubbell’s materials have described that a relatively small share of U.S. attorneys meet the AV Preeminent band.

Avvo, Lawyers.com, and other directories

Independent public profiles (Avvo, Lawyers.com, and similar) publish client and peer information on their terms. Interfaces and URLs change; the authoritative listing is the live profile, not a screenshot. Client ratings on consumer platforms reflect the reviewers who chose to post, not a census of all clients.

Expertise, regional “best” lists, and other publishers

From time to time, publishers and aggregators (including Expertise and similar services) list attorneys in a geographic or practice category—for example, coverage that references a market such as Bethlehem. Inclusion, naming, and ranking are controlled entirely by the publisher, can change, and are not a court judgment or bar endorsement. No separate Expertise graphic is displayed on this site at this time. If you have an official badge file from the publisher, it can be added in the same row as the graphics above, subject to the publisher’s license terms.

“Top 100” and similar lists

Where a biography references selection among “Top 100” criminal defense attorneys, the meaning and selection criteria are set by the publisher, not the firm, and the firm can explain the context in a consultation.

Results in past cases do not guarantee a particular outcome in a new case.
