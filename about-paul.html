About Paul A. Bauer III, Esq.

Criminal and DUI defense for Pennsylvanians, based in the Pottstown area. Appointments are scheduled — call the office to reach the team.

Credentials that matter in court

Paul is a former prosecutor in Montgomery County, where he was promoted and later served as Chief of the Major Crimes Division. He has taken a high volume of cases to court — including capital murder matters as a prosecutor — and he brings that trial experience to defense work in DUI and serious criminal cases.

Peer review, not a mail-order “badge”

AV Preeminent (Martindale-Hubbell) is a confidential peer review process. It is not something a firm can simply purchase, and it is one of several tools clients use to understand reputation among attorneys and judges — alongside results, fit, and how a case is actually prepared.

How a serious file is read

A defense file is not a narrative you tell once—it is a set of materials that have to cohere: charging papers, body or dash video when it exists, testing and calibration records, and the calendar of court and administrative deadlines.

Education

B.S. in accounting, Villanova University (1989); M.B.A., Widener University Graduate School of Business; J.D., Widener University School of Law.

Prosecutor background and trial experience

Paul began in the Montgomery County District Attorney’s office, was promoted, and later served as Chief of the Major Crimes Division. As a prosecutor he tried more than 1,000 cases, including capital murder matters—experience he carries into defense work in DUI and serious criminal cases in Pennsylvania.

He has also been selected by his peers in multiple years among the Top 100 criminal defense attorneys in the state (per the publisher’s criteria in those years). His AV Preeminent Martindale-Hubbell peer rating is a confidential bar-and-bench review, not a consumer poll.

Bar membership and current focus

Long-time member in good standing in the Montgomery and Berks County bar associations. Today, the practice is criminal defense with a high volume of DUI and criminal matters—Pottstown-based, with clients from surrounding areas.

Clarity is the point. A consultation is a working conversation about what you are charged with, what you have in hand, and what comes next—not a performance.

How the firm works with clients

Serious cases require more than a webpage. The firm is prepared to walk the timeline, the documents, the testing, and the defenses that fit your facts—without slogans, and with room for hard questions first.
